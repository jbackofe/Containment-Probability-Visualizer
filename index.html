Probability of Circle Containment Under Positional Uncertainty

A circle has a center whose position follows a 2D normal (Gaussian) distribution. Another circle can be placed at one of N equally spaced positions on a ring.

This simulation estimates the probability that the randomly positioned circle would be fully contained within one of those possible positions.

What counts as a success?
Success if mink ||X - ck|| ≤ R - r

Here, X is the sampled center of the randomly positioned circle, and ck is one allowed center for the other circle.

Each allowed position creates a green containment region of radius R − r. If a sampled center lands inside any green region, full containment occurs.

What this simulation is doing

Step 1: Draw many possible center locations for the randomly positioned circle from the Gaussian distribution.

Step 2: For each sampled center, check whether it falls inside at least one green containment region.

Estimated probability = successful sampled centers / total sampled centers

The yellow circles show the actual possible positions of the second circle. The green regions are smaller because the random circle’s center must stay within distance R − r of an allowed center to fit completely inside.
